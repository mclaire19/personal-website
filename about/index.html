About Me

Broadly

My main interests include data visualization, teaching and outreach in the data science community, and social policy. That said, I’m a real nerd, and I tend to get excited about all manner of cool projects and topics. I find all forms of communication fascinating and important, and I believe very strongly that advanced data insights mean nothing if they can’t be conveyed to the audiences who matter. If you want to learn more about my work, check out projects I’ve worked on, my Github, or my blog.

More Specifically

I graduated in May 2020 from the Georgetown McCourt School of Public Policy with an M.S. in Data Science for Public Policy - a relatively new degree that was designed to target the intersection of policy and data analytics. For a two-year program, they did an amazing job - we walked out with a healthy understand of R and Python, machine learning models (and their limitations!) and the uniqueness of working with data in the policy space. That said, I also walked out feeling incredibly overwhelmed by everything I didn’t know or understand. I’ve been really grateful to the open source community, especially the RStats community, for providing free labor and content that lets me grow in my skills and understanding, and I’m working to get to the point where I can contribute too.

I currently work as a data analyst at the Universal Service Administrative Company (USAC), a nonprofit subsidiary of the FCC that oversees programs dedicated to improving broadband access in the US. In this job, I work with company-wide data to communicate insights and trends to executives via reports and ad hoc data pulls, using primarily R, SQL, and Tableau.

I’m also currently a Teaching Assistant (TA) for a graduate-level introductory data science course for MPP students. I previously worked as a TA for two semesters of graduate-level statistics, and created materials for a weekly recitation covering applications of the course material in R. I’ve really loved these roles for letting me work with students and refine my own understanding of R; they inspired a few of my blog posts on Troubleshooting in R for Beginners and How to Google Your Errors.

Everything Else

I’ve been in school (and now quarantine) for so long that I’m still working on figuring out hobbies. One thing that has stayed consistent is that I love reading anything and everything, from fantasy novels to autobiographies to, yes, technical discussions of data visualization software. I’ve recently been dabbling in very bad watercolor and very beginner-level yoga. I own way too many mugs, but am always ready to buy one more.
